September 28 Jacob Lurie (Harvard) Equivariant Cohomology via Algebraic Groups

I will explain how the study of group schemes in derived algebraic geometry leads naturally to the construction of equivariant cohomology theories. In particular, I will sketch a purely algebraic construction of equivariant K-theory and provide an interpretation of Snaith's theorem, which relates complex K-theory to the suspension spectrum of CP^{infty}.
October 5 Nick Kuhn (UVa) Introduction to the Telescope Conjecture II
October 12 Kai-Uwe Bux (UVa) Connectivity of Spheres and Horospheres in Euclidean Buildings

Let $X$ be a Euclidean building of dimension $m$. The Tits-Solomon theorem implies that metric spheres of small diameter are $(m-1)$-spherical. We show that this generalizes to metric spheres of arbitrary size. If $X$ is irreducible, we want to show that horospheres are also $(m-2)$-connected, and we may already have a proof. This is joint with Kevin Wortman (Yale).

March 15 Soren Galatius (Stanford) Homotopy Type of the Cobordism Category

The d-dimensional cobordism category C_d has closed (d-1)-dimensional manifolds as objects and compact d-dimensional cobordisms as morphisms. Thom's theorem determines pi_0 of the classifying space BC_d. I will discuss joint work with Madsen, Tillmann and Weiss, in which we determine the homotopy type of BC_d. As a corollary we get a new proof of Madsen-Weiss' generalized Mumford conjecture. arXiv
