Complex Analysis General Exam - January 2020

Problem 1

Compute

limR→∞∫−RRsin⁡(x)xdx\lim _{R \rightarrow \infty} \int_{-R}^{R} \frac{\sin (x)}{x} d x

using Residue theory.

Problem 2

Let ff be a nonconstant entire function. Show that f(ℂ)f(\mathbb{C}) is dense in ℂ\mathbb{C}. You are not allowed to use any of the Picard’s theorem.

Problem 3

Let ff be an entire function. Assume there is a sequence rn∈(0,∞)r_{n} \in(0, \infty) with rn→∞r_{n} \rightarrow \infty, and constants C,α∈(0,∞)C, \alpha \in(0, \infty) with

supz∈ℂ:|z|=rn|f(z)|≤Crnα\sup _{z \in \mathbb{C}:|z|=r_{n}}|f(z)| \leq C r_{n}^{\alpha}

Show that ff is a polynomial.

Problem 4

Let 𝔻={z∈ℂ:|z|<1}\mathbb{D}=\{z \in \mathbb{C}:|z|<1\}. Let (fn)n\left(f_{n}\right)_{n} be a sequence of holomorphic functions and suppose that fnf_{n} converge uniformly on compact subsets of 𝔻\mathbb{D} to a function ff. Suppose ff has no zeroes on {z∈ℂ:|z|=12}\left\{z \in \mathbb{C}:|z|=\frac{1}{2}\right\}. Show that there exists an N∈ℕN \in \mathbb{N} so that if n,m≥Nn, m \geq N, then fn,fmf_{n}, f_{m} have the same number of zeroes in {z∈ℂ:|z|<1/2}\{z \in \mathbb{C}:|z|<1 / 2\} (counting with multiplicity). (This is Hurwitz’s theorem, you are not allowed to just quote this theorem).

Problem 5

Let UU be an open subset of ℂ\mathbb{C}. Let fn:U→ℂf_{n}: U \rightarrow \mathbb{C} be a sequence of analytic functions. Suppose that fnf_{n} converge pointwise to a function f:U→ℂf: U \rightarrow \mathbb{C}. If |fn|≤1\left|f_{n}\right| \leq 1 for every nn, show that ff is analytic.
