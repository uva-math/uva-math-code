Real Analysis, September 1984

  1. Let ff and gg be Lebesgue measurable functions on ℝn\mathbb{R}^{n}. Consider the hypotheses:
    (i) f,gf, g are both in L1(ℝn)L^{1}\left(\mathbb{R}^{n}\right),
    (ii) f,gf, g are both in L2(ℝn)L^{2}\left(\mathbb{R}^{n}\right),
    (iii) ff is in L1(ℝn),gL^{1}\left(\mathbb{R}^{n}\right), g is in L∞(ℝn)L^{\infty}\left(\mathbb{R}^{n}\right).
    (a) Which of the hypotheses imply fgf g is in L1(ℝn)L^{1}\left(\mathbb{R}^{n}\right) ?
    (b) For those hypotheses which do not imply fgf g is in L1(ℝn)L^{1}\left(\mathbb{R}^{n}\right) give a counter-example.

F(x)=∑ixi≤xpiF(x)=\sum_{\substack{i \\ x_{i} \leq x}} p_{i}

and let μF\mu_{F} denote Lebesgue-Stieltjes measure with respect to FF. Show that
(i) Every function f:ℝ→ℝf: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} is μF\mu_{F}-measurable,
(ii) ff is integrable if and only if ∑ipif(xi)\sum_{i} p_{i} f\left(x_{i}\right) converges absolutely.

(i) The rational numbers QQ are a GδG_{\delta} in ℝ\mathbb{R}.

4. State and prove the Riemann-Lebesgue lemma for functions on the circle.
