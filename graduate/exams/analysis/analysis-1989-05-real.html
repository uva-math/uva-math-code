Real Analysis, May 1989

  1. (a) If μ\mu is a finite measure on a space Ω\Omega and 1≤r≤s<∞1 \leq r \leq s<\infty, show that Ls(Ω,μ)⊆Lr(Ω,μ)L^{s}(\Omega, \mu) \subseteq L^{r}(\Omega, \mu).
    (b) Prove or disprove that the assertion is true without the assumption that the measure is finite.

  2. (a) Let f1,f2,…f_{1}, f_{2}, \ldots and ff be continuous, real-valued functions on a closed and bounded interval [a,b][a, b] such that fn→ff_{n} \rightarrow f uniformly on [a,b][a, b]. Prove or disprove that fn2→f2f_{n}^{2} \rightarrow f^{2} uniformly on [a,b][a, b].
    (b) What happens if [a,b][a, b] is replaced by (−∞,∞)(-\infty, \infty) ?

  3. (a) Let ( Ω,ℱ,μ\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mu ) be a finite measure space and ℬ\mathcal{B} a sub- σ\sigma-field of ℱ\mathcal{F}. Show that if ff is a real-valued function in L1(Ω,ℱ,μ)L^{1}(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mu), then there exists a ℬ\mathcal{B}-measurable function hh on Ω\Omega such that

∫Bfdμ=∫Bhdμ\int_{B} f d \mu=\int_{B} h d \mu

for all B∈ℬB \in \mathcal{B}.
(b) Find the function hh if Ω\Omega is [0,1][0,1] with Lebesgue measure ℬ\mathcal{B} is the σ\sigma-field generated by the two intervals [0,1/2)[0,1 / 2) and [1/2,1][1 / 2,1], and f(x)=x2f(x)=x^{2} for all xx.
4. Let ff and gg be two functions in L2(−∞,∞)L^{2}(-\infty, \infty).
(a) Show that

limx→0∫−∞∞|g(t+x)−g(t)|2dt=0\lim _{x \rightarrow 0} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}|g(t+x)-g(t)|^{2} d t=0

(b) Show that

F(x)=∫−∞∞f(t)g(x−t)dtF(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t) g(x-t) d t

defines a continuous function on (−∞,∞)(-\infty, \infty).
