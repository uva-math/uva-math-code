Real Analysis General Exam January 2022

Instructions. 2 hours. Closed book examination. Be neat in your presentation. When invoking a theorem from previous courses, name the theorem and thoroughly check its hypotheses. You must solve a significant portion of each of the three problems in order to pass the exam.

Problem 1

Let ( X,ℳ,μX, \mathscr{M}, \mu ) be a measure space. Let ( fnf_{n} ) be a sequence of nonnegative functions functions in L1(X,ℳ,μ)L^{1}(X, \mathscr{M}, \mu) and let ff be a nonnegative function in L1(X,ℳ,μ)L^{1}(X, \mathscr{M}, \mu). Suppose that

∫Xfndμ→∫Xfdμ\int_{X} f_{n} \mathrm{~d} \mu \longrightarrow \int_{X} f \mathrm{~d} \mu

and that fn→ff_{n} \rightarrow f pointwise. Prove that fnf_{n} converges to ff in L1(X,ℳ,μ)L^{1}(X, \mathscr{M}, \mu). Hint: consider gn=min⁡(f,fn)g_{n}=\min \left(f, f_{n}\right).
