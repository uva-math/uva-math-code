Solve as many problems as you can. Full solutions on a smaller number of problems will be worth more than partial solutions on several problems.

limn→∞∫Xgn(x)dμ(x)=∫Xg(x)dμ(x)\lim _{n \rightarrow \infty} \int_{X} g_{n}(x) d \mu(x)=\int_{X} g(x) d \mu(x)

Problem 2

(a) Let (X,ℳ,μ)(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu) be a finite measure space. Show that if p,p′∈[1,∞]p, p^{\prime} \in[1, \infty] with p<p′p<p^{\prime}, then Lp(X,ℳ,μ)⊇Lp′(X,ℳ,μ)L^{p}(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu) \supseteq L^{p^{\prime}}(X, \mathcal{M}, \mu).
(b) Show that if p,p′∈[1,∞]p, p^{\prime} \in[1, \infty] with p<p′p<p^{\prime}, the spaces Lp(ℝ)∖Lp′(ℝ)L^{p}(\mathbb{R}) \backslash L^{p^{\prime}}(\mathbb{R}) and Lp′(ℝ)∖Lp(ℝ)L^{p^{\prime}}(\mathbb{R}) \backslash L^{p}(\mathbb{R}) are both nonempty. (Note that the Lebesgue measure on ℝ\mathbb{R} is not a finite measure, but merely a σ\sigma-finite measure.)
