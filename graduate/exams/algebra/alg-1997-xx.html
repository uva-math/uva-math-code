Algebra General Examination - Saturday, August 23, 1997; 9-12

This examination is closed book. Please explain yourself carefully and clearly; avoid words like "obviously"! Save your scratch-work and turn it in along with the examination. Allow enough time to polish your answers. There are eight problems.

  1. Let pp be a prime number, and let KK be a field containing pp distinct pp-th roots of unity. Let L/KL / K be a Galois extension for which [L:K]=p[L: K]=p.
    a) Prove that the Galois group Gal⁡(L/K)\operatorname{Gal}(L / K) is cyclic of order pp.
    b) Let σ\sigma be a generator of Gal⁡(L/K)\operatorname{Gal}(L / K). Then σ\sigma is a KK-linear transformation on the vector space LL. What is the Jordan canonical form of σ\sigma ?
    c) Prove that there is an element uu in L−KL-K with upu^{p} in KK.

  2. a) Let GG be a finite group with exactly two conjugacy classes of elements. Prove that |G|=2|G|=2, where |G||G| is the order of GG.
    b) Suppose that GG has exactly three conjugacy classes of elements. Show that |G||G| involves at most two primes.
    c) There are, in fact, only two finite groups with exactly three conjugacy classes of elements. Can you guess which ones they are?

  3. Let K(x)K(x) be the field of rational functions over the field KK. Prove Lüroth’s theorem: for any nonconstant function, ff (that is, f∈K(x)−Kf \in K(x)-K ), the degree [K(x):K(f)][K(x): K(f)] is finite. Can you describe [K(x):K(f)][K(x): K(f)] in terms of ff ?

  4. Let f(x)f(x) be a monic polynomial in ℤ[x]\mathbb{Z}[x]. Let pp be a prime and let f‾(x)\bar{f}(x) be the image of f(x)f(x) in (ℤ/pℤ)[x](\mathbb{Z} / p \mathbb{Z})[x] obtained by reducing the coefficients of f(x)f(x) modulo p. A theorem of Kronecker says that if f‾(x)\bar{f}(x) has distinct roots in its splitting field, and if σ‾\bar{\sigma} is a member of the Galois group of f‾(x)\bar{f}(x) over ℤ/pℤ\mathbb{Z} / p \mathbb{Z}, then there is an element σ\sigma of the Galois group of f(x)f(x) over ℚ\mathbb{Q} having the same cycle structure as a permutation of the roots of f(x)f(x) as σ‾\bar{\sigma} does as a permutation of the roots of f‾(x)\bar{f}(x).
    a) Use this theorem (with a couple of choices of pp ) to show that the Galois group of x4+4x2+x+3x^{4}+4 x^{2}+x+3 is isomorphic to S4S_{4}.
    b) Apply the theorem to show that if f(x)f(x) has even degree and the discriminant of f(x)f(x) is a perfect square in ℤ\mathbb{Z}, then f‾(x)\bar{f}(x) is reducible for each prime pp.

  5. If gg is a member of a group GG, then gg is called a nongenerator of GG if whenever GG is generated by a subset containing gg, it is also generated by the subset with
    gg removed. It is a fact that the set of all nongenerators of GG forms a subgroup; that subgroup is called the Frattini subgroup of GG.

Suppose that GG is a finite pp-group, pp a prime, and FF is the Frattini subgroup of GG. Show that g∈Fg \in F if and only if gg is in every subgroup of index pp of GG. (You may need the fact that if HH is a proper subgroup of GG, then HH is a proper subgroup of its normalizer in GG.) Conclude that G/FG / F is an Abelian group of exponent pp.
6. Let RR be an integral domain and let N:R−{0}→{n>0∣n∈ℤ}N: R-\{0\} \rightarrow\{n>0 \mid n \in \mathbb{Z}\} be a function for which N(1)=1N(1)=1 and N(xy)=N(x)N(y)N(x y)=N(x) N(y), for all x,yx, y in R−{0}R-\{0\}.
a) Let KK be the field of fractions of RR. Show that NN can be extended in a unique way to a function from K−{0}K-\{0\} into ℚ\mathbb{Q} for which N(xy)=N(x)N(y)N(x y)=N(x) N(y) still holds.
b) Show that RR is a Euclidean domain (under NN ) if and only if for each x∈K−{0}x \in K-\{0\} there is an element r∈Rr \in R for which N(x−r)<1N(x-r)<1.
