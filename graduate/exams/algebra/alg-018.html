General Exam-Algebra
August 14th 14{ }^{\text {th }}, 2000

  1. Let V be a non-zero vector space over the field of reals R . Show that V cannot be presented as the union of three proper subspaces.

  2. For a field KK, we denote by K(x)K(x) the field of rational functions in one variable xx over KK. Prove that C(x)C(x) is not the algebraic closure of R(x)R(x). ( RR is the real numbers and CC the complex numbers.)

  3. (i). Let G be a finite group of order pam\mathrm{p}^{a} \mathrm{~m} where p is a prime relatively prime to m , and let Gp\mathrm{G}_{\mathrm{p}} denote the set of elements of GG whose order is pip^{i} for some i>0i>0. Show that
    |Gp|≤[G:N](pα−1)\left|G_{p}\right| \leq[G: N]\left(p^{\alpha}-1\right) where NN is the normalizer of some Sylow pp-subgroup, with equality holding iff any two distinct Sylow p-subgroups have trivial intersection.
    (ii). Let G be a finite group of order pαqβ\mathrm{p}^{\alpha} \mathrm{q}^{\beta} for distinct primes p and q . Assume that G does not have elements of order pq , and that G has just 1 Sylow q -subgroup.

Show that |Gp|=qβ(pα−1)\left|G_{p}\right|=q^{\beta}\left(p^{\alpha}-1\right).
Show that any two distinct Sylow p-subgroups have only the identity in common.
4. Let A denote the set of complex numbers of the form a+b−6\mathrm{a}+\mathrm{b} \sqrt{-6}, with a,b∈Z\mathrm{a}, \mathrm{b} \in \mathrm{Z}.
(i) Show that A is a subring of C .
(ii) Let J be the ideal of A generated by 5 and 2+−62+\sqrt{-6}. Show that J≠A\mathrm{J} \neq \mathrm{A}.
(iii) Show that J is prime.
(iv) Show that the ideal 5A is not prime.
5. Let A be a commutative ring with 1 . Show that A[x,x−1]\mathrm{A}\left[\mathrm{x}, \mathrm{x}^{-1}\right] is Noetherian if and only if A is. (Reminder. A Noetherian ring is one in which every ideal is finitely generated. You may use any characterization of a Noetherian ring you know; you do not need to justify it.)
6. Let R be a ring with 1 and suppose that N is a non-zero left R -module. Show that N is indecomposable if and only if Hom⁡R(N,N)\operatorname{Hom}_{R}(N, N) does not contain an idempotent e with e≠0\mathrm{e} \neq 0 and e≠1\mathrm{e} \neq 1.
(Reminders. A module is indecomposable if it not the direct sum of two non-zero submodules. An element ss of a ring is idempotent if s2=ss^{2}=s.)
7. Let K be a Galois extension of Q , the rational numbers, with the Galois group G(K:Q)\mathrm{G}(\mathrm{K}: \mathrm{Q}) isomorphic to A4\mathrm{A}_{4}. ( A4\mathrm{A}_{4} is the group of even permutations of 4 objects.)
(i) What is the degree of K over Q ?
(ii) Show that there is a polynomial ff of degree 4 so that KK is isomorphic to the splitting field of ff over Q.
Let r1,r2,r3,r4r_{1}, r_{2}, r_{3}, r_{4} be the roots of ff in KK. Let Δ=∏∏1≤i<j≤4(ri−rj)\Delta=\prod \prod_{1 \leqslant i<j \leqslant 4}\left(r_{i}-r_{j}\right).
(iii) Show that Δ2\Delta^{2} is in Q .
(iv) Is Δ\Delta in Q ? Why or why not?
8. A certain ZZ-module MM is generated by elements a,ba, b, and cc. Furthermore 2a+4b+2c=02 a+4 b+2 c=0, 4a+4b+4c=04 a+4 b+4 c=0, and 2a+4b+14c=02 a+4 b+14 c=0. Assume all other ZZ-combinations of a,b,ca, b, c which are 0 can be derived from these three. (i.e. these are the defining relations for the module.) Find the invariant factors for this module and describe M up to isomorphism as a direct sum of cyclic submodules.
9. Let X be an invertible matrix with complex entries and let Y=X−1\mathrm{Y}=\mathrm{X}^{-1}.
(i). Show that if λ\lambda is an eigenvalue for X then λ−1\lambda^{-1} is an eigenvalue for Y .
(ii). Show that if the Jordan canonical form of X has a kxk Jordan block with diagonal entries λ\lambda, then the Jordan canonical form of Y has a kxk Jordan block with diagonal entries λ−1\lambda^{-1}.
