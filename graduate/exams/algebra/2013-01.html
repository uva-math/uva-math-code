Algebra general exam. January 9, 2013, 9am -1pm

Directions.

  • Please show all your work and justify any statements that you make.

  • State clearly and fully any theorem you use.

  • Vague statements and hand-waving arguments will not be appreciated.

  • You may assume the statement in an earlier part proven in order to do a later part.

Problem 1

Let pp be a prime and let S2pS_{2 p} denote the symmetric group on 2p2 p elements.
(a) ( 2 pts) Find the order of a pp-Sylow subgroup of S2pS_{2 p}.
(b) ( 5 pts) Describe explicitly a pp-Sylow subgroup of S2pS_{2 p} (providing a generating set counts as explicit description, but make sure to prove that your subgroup is indeed pp-Sylow).
(c) ( 2 pts) Consider the set of elements of order pp in S2pS_{2 p} - clearly, it is a union of conjugacy classes. How many conjugacy classes does it consist of?
(d) ( 5 pts) Now consider the set of elements of order pp in the alternating group A2pA_{2 p}. How many conjugacy classes (of A2pA_{2 p} ) does it consist of? Make sure to justify your answer.
Hint: Distinguish between the cases p=2p=2 and p>2p>2.
