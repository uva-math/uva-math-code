Algebra General Exam

  1. Show (by Sylow theory) that a group of order 72 cannot be simple. List all abelian groups of order 72.

  2. Let GG be an infinite group containing an element x≠1x \neq 1 having only finitely many conjugates. Prove that GG is not simple.

  3. Let f(x)=x4−x2−2∈q[x]f(x)=x^{4}-x^{2}-2 \in q[x]. Find the splitting field FF of f(x)f(x) over QQ, determine its Galois group, and find all subfields of FF.

  4. Let k⊆K⊆Lk \subseteq K \subseteq L and let α∈L\alpha \in L. Assume that K/KK / K is finite algebraic.
    (a) Show that K/KK / K separable =K(α)/K(α)=K(\alpha) / K(\alpha) separable.
    (b) Show that K/KK / K normal ⇒K(α)/K(α)\Rightarrow K(\alpha) / K(\alpha) normal.
    (c) Show that if K/KK / K is Galois, then so is K(α)/K(α)K(\alpha) / K(\alpha) and compare the Galois groups G(K/k)G(K / k) and G(K(α)/K(α))G(K(\alpha) / K(\alpha)).

  5. What are necessary and sufficient conditions on the commutative integral domain RR in order that the polynomial ring in one indeterminate, R[x]R[x], be
    (a) a P.I.D.?
    (b) a UFD?
    (c) a Noetherian ring?

  6. Let RR be a commutative ring with unit and II an ideal of RR. Consider the RR-module R/IR / I.
    (a) Show that if II is a prime ideal, then R/IR / I is indecomposable.
    (b) Show that R/IR / I is a simple module if and ouly if II is maximal.

  7. Let TT be a linear transformation of ℝ5\mathbb{R}^{5} having characteristic polynomial T5−TT^{5}-T. Find its possible rational canonical forms over ℝ\mathbb{R} and its possible Jordan canonical forms over ℂ\mathbb{C}.
