ALGEBRA GENERAL EXAMINATION

January 28, 1995

This examination is closed book. Please write your answers out neatly and include all vour scratch work. The time allowed is three hours. and there are eight problems.

  1. In keeping with tradition, let GG be a group of order 1995. Show that GG must be solvable. (Although you needn’t prove them here, you may use only theorems you are able to prove!)

  2. Explain the connection between the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic and the Jordan-Hölder Theorem applied to cyclic groups.

  3. Let KK and LL be finite extensions of a field FF, both contained in a field EE. Let KLK L be the set of sums of products of members of KK and LL.

Explain why KLK L is a subring of EE that is finite dimensional over FF. From that, show that KLK L is a field and, in fact, the smallest subfield of EE containing KK and LL.
4. List all the possible Jordan canonical forms for a matrix over the complex field whose characteristic polynomial is x8−x4x^{8}-x^{4} and whose minimal polynomial is x6−x2x^{6}-x^{2}.
5. Give definitions of the terms "maximal subgroup" and "minimal subgroup" - it is not assumed that you have seen these terms explicitly. Then from your definitions, prove the following facts:
a) A minimal subgroup must be cyclic of prime order.
b) If a subgroup has prime index, it is a maximal subgroup.
c) If a subgroup is both maximal and normal, it has prime index.
d) A subgroup of an Abelian group is maximal if and only if it has prime index.

Now find the maximal and minimal subgroups of ℤ\mathbb{Z}.
6. Prove that any proper homomorphic image of a principal ideal domain that is an integral domain must actually be a field.

Use this result to show that F[x,y],FF[x, y], F a field, is not a principal ideal domain.
7. Show that x4−2x2−2x^{4}-2 x^{2}-2 is irreducible over ℚ\mathbb{Q}. Label its roots in some way and display the Galois group as a set of permutations of the roots.
8. Give an example of a polynomial f(x)f(x) in ℚ[x]\mathbb{Q}[x] having all these properties:
a) the degree of f(x)f(x) is 4 ;
b) f(x)f(x) has no rational roots;
c) f(x)f(x) has no repeated factors in ℚ[x]\mathbb{Q}[x];
d) the Galois group of f(x)f(x) over ℚ\mathbb{Q} is cyclic of order 2 .
