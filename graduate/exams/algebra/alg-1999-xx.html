Algebra General Exam August 21, 1999

This is exam is worth 100 points. The number of points each part of a problem is worth is indicated in brackets. If you can’t prove part of some problem, you can still use the result of that part in proving subsequent parts.
(1)[10] Let PP be a pp-Sylow subgroup of a finite group GG for some prime pp dividing the group order. We know by Sylow theory that the index of the normalizer of PP is congruent to 1 modulo pp,

[G:NG(P)]≡1(mod⁡p)\left[G: N_{G}(P)\right] \equiv 1(\bmod p)

Show that the same holds for any larger subgroup MM : if G⊇M⊇NG(P)G \supseteq M \supseteq N_{G}(P) then

[G:M]≡1(mod⁡p)[G: M] \equiv 1(\bmod p)

[Hint: show NM(P)=NG(P)N_{M}(P)=N_{G}(P).]
(2)[20] (a)[5] Show that any finite group GG has a faithful action on some set SS of cardinality |S|=|G||S|=|G|.
From now on (in parts b,c\mathrm{b}, \mathrm{c} ) assume that GG is a finite pp-group for a prime pp, having the property that it has a unique subgroup GpG_{p} of order pp. [The quaternion group is such a group, with p=2p=2 and G2={1,−1}G_{2}=\{1,-1\}.]
(b)[5] Show that GpG_{p} is invariant under all endomorphisms of G,f(Gp)⊆GpG, f\left(G_{p}\right) \subseteq G_{p} for all homomorphisms f:G→Gf: G \rightarrow G.
(c)[10] Show that GpG_{p} "needs room" in order to act: whenever GG acts on a finite set SS of size |S|<|G||S|<|G|, the subgroup GpG_{p} acts trivially. Conclude that GG can only act faithfully on sets of size ≥|G|\geq|G|.
(3)[10] The exponent ee of a group GG is defined as the smallest positive integer kk such that xk=1x^{k}=1 for all x∈Gx \in G; for an abelian group, in additive notation this is the smallest kk such that kx=0k x=0 for all elements. If n1,n2,…,nrn_{1}, n_{2}, \ldots, n_{r} are the invariant factors of a finite abelian group AA (so nr|nr−1|…|n2|n1n_{r}\left|n_{r-1}\right| \ldots\left|n_{2}\right| n_{1} ), prove that AA has exponent e=n1e=n_{1}, and has an element of order precisely ee. Conclude that AA has an element of order mm iff mm divides the largest invariant factor n1n_{1}.
