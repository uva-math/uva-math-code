5. Let AA be a finite-dimensional algebra over the field, ℂ\mathbb{C}, of complex numbers. What can you say about AA in the following cases?
(a) AA is simple.
(b) AA is semi-simple.
(c) Let T:V→VT: V \rightarrow V be a linear transformation on the finite-dimensional vector space over ℂ\mathbb{C}. Let A=ℂ[T]A=\mathbb{C}[T] be the algebra of polynomials in TT over ℂ\mathbb{C}. When is AA a simple algebra?
(d) When is the algebra of part (c) semi-simple and what can you say about TT in this case?

Note: In parts (c) and (d), your answer should specify conditions on the transformation T.
