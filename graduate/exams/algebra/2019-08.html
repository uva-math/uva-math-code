Problem 5

Let FF be a field, let VV be a vector space over FF of finite dimension nn, and let T:V→VT: V \rightarrow V be an FF-linear map.
(a) ( 5 pts) Assume that FF is algebraically closed. Prove that VV has at least n+1Tn+1 T-invariant subspaces (including 0 and VV )
(b) ( 2 pts) Give an example showing that if FF is not algebraically closed, the conclusion of (a) may be false.
(c) ( 7 pts) Now assume that TT is diagonalizable over FF and has nn distinct eigenvalues. Prove that the number of TT-invariant subspaces depends only on nn and find that number.
