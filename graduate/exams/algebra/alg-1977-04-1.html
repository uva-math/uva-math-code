Algebra, April 1977

  1. Let CC be the field of complex numbers, and let AA be an n×nn \times n matrix over CC. As usual, we define eA=∑k=0∞Akk!=I+A+12!A2+13!A3+⋯e^{A}=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{A^{k}}{k!}=I+A+\frac{1}{2!} A^{2}+\frac{1}{3!} A^{3}+\cdots, where the convergence of the series is that of Cn2C^{n^{2}}.
    (a) Use the Jordan canonical form to show that det⁡eA=eTrA\operatorname{det} e^{A}=e^{T r A}.
    (b) If A=(1101)A=\left(\begin{array}{ll}1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{array}\right), what is eAe^{A} ?

  2. Let kk be a field of qq elements where qq is finite.
    (a) Show that q=pnq=p^{n} is a power of a prime.
    (b) Show that kk is the splitting field of the equation xq−x=0x^{q}-x=0 and thus is the unique field of qq elements.
    (c) The multiplicative group k*k^{*} of non-zero elements of kk satisfy the equation xq−1−1=x^{q-1}-1= 0 . Why does this imply that k*k^{*} is a cyclic group of order q−1q-1 ?

  3. Use the fundamental theorem for abelian groups to show that in the ring of integers mod pnp^{n}, for pp an odd prime, the multiplicative group of integers relatively prime to pp has a subgroup of order pp if n>1n>1.

  4. Let RR be a commutative ring with 1 and let MM be a maximal ideal in RR. Show that R/MR / M is a field.
    (a) Show that if RR is a Euclidean Integral Domain then every ideal generated by an irreducible element is maximal.
    (b) Let kk be a field and R=k[x]R=k[x] the polynomial ring in one variable over kk. If p(x)=pp(x)=p is an irreducible polynomial in k[x]k[x] use 4 . and (a) to show that there is a finite extension KK of kk in which p(x)p(x) has a root.
    (c) If k=Qk=Q is the rational field and p(x)=x2+x+1p(x)=x^{2}+x+1 show that p(x)p(x) is indeed irreducible over QQ. What is the field generated in (b) for this example?

  5. Let G=SnG=S_{n} be the symmetric group on nn letters.
    (a) Show that the alternating group AnA_{n} on nn letters is a normal subgroup of SnS_{n}.
    (b) Show that AnA_{n} is generated by the three-cycles (1,2,i),i=3,4,…,n(1,2, i), i=3,4, \ldots, n.
    (c) Show that if HH is a normal subgroup of AnA_{n} for n≥3n \geq 3 and HH contains a 3-cycle, then H=AnH=A_{n}.

  6. Let k=Qk=Q be the rational field and let f(x)=x3+5f(x)=x^{3}+5.
    (a) Show that f(x)f(x) is irreducible over QQ.
    (b) Use Galois theory to show that the Galois group of f(x)f(x) cannot be of order 3.
    (c) Find the Galois group of f(x)f(x).
    (d) Is the equation f(x)f(x) solvable by radical? Give reasons for your answer.

  7. Let AA be a normal (A*A=AA*)n×n\left(A^{*} A=A A^{*}\right) n \times n complex matrix. Show that there is a polynomial f(x)f(x) with complex coefficients such that A*=f(A)A^{*}=f(A). Thus any n×nn \times n matrix BB which commutes with AA also commutes with A*A^{*}. (Assume the spectral theorem for AA.)

  8. If AA and BB are algebras over a field kk define their tensor product C=A⊗kBC=A \otimes_{k} B and show that CC is an algebra over kk.
    (a) Let AA and BB each have units and let Z(A)=Z1,Z(B)=Z2Z(A)=Z_{1}, Z(B)=Z_{2}, and Z(C)=ZZ(C)=Z designate the centers of A,BA, B, and CC respectively. Show that Z=Z1⊗Z2Z=Z_{1} \otimes Z_{2}.
