ALGEBRA GENERAL EXAMINATION

August 24, 1996

Instructions. This algebra examination is closed book. Please write out your answers carefully, being sure to explain any claims you make. Include your scratch work with what you turn in. The time allowed is three hours, and there are eight questions.

  1. A Hall subgroup HH of a finite group GG is a subgroup whose order and index are relatively prime. Use isomorphism theorems to prove that if NN is a normal subgroup of GG and HH is a Hall subgroup of GG, then HN/NH N / N is a Hall subgroup of G/NG / N, and H∩NH \cap N is a Hall subgroup of NN.

  2. Let CnC_{n} denote the cyclic group of order nn. Determine all pairs of positive integers aa and bb, with a≤ba \leq b, for which Ca×CbC_{a} \times C_{b} is isomorphic to C15×C18×C20C_{15} \times C_{18} \times C_{20}.

  3. Let MM be a finitely generated RR-module, RR a ring. Show that any generating set of MM (a subset of MM that generates MM as an RR-module) must contain a finite generating set. Conclude that MM has a minimal generating set (no proper subset generates MM ), and that every minimal generating set of MM is finite.

  4. In Q[x]\mathrm{Q}[x], let f(x)=xm1+…+xmkf(x)=x^{m_{1}}+\ldots+x^{m_{k}}, where mi≡i−1(mod⁡k)m_{i} \equiv i-1(\bmod k). Show that f(x)f(x) is divisible by xk−1+xk−2+…+1x^{k-1}+x^{k-2}+\ldots+1.

  5. Let RR be a principal ideal domain. An ideal PP of RR is called primary if whenever ab∈Pa b \in P and a∉Pa \notin P, then bn∈Pb^{n} \in P for some nn (perhaps depending on bb ). Show that PP is a primary ideal if and only if either P={0}P=\{0\} or P=(pm)P=\left(p^{m}\right) for some prime pp and exponent mm.

  6. Let E/FE / F be a finite Galois extension with Galois group GG. The normal basis theorem states that there is an element uu in EE whose images under the members of GG form an FF-basis of EE.

Prove that for any subgroup HH of GG, the subfield corresponding to HH in the Galois correspondence is F(uH)F\left(u_{H}\right), where

uH=∑h∈Hh(u)u_{H}=\sum_{h \in H} h(u)

  1. The minimal polynomial of a matrix AA with real entries is (x−1)(x2+1)2(x-1)\left(x^{2}+1\right)^{2} and its characteristic polynomial is (x−1)3(x2+1)2(x-1)^{3}\left(x^{2}+1\right)^{2}.
    (a) What is the rational canonical form of AA ?
    (b) If AA is regarded as having complex entries, what is its rational canonical form?
    (c) What is the Jordan normal form of AA (again, with complex entries)?

  2. Give an example of two finite groups whose Sylow subgroups are isomorphic for each prime, but which are themselves not isomorphic.
