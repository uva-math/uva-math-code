Algebra General Exam

August 18, 2001
(1) If ϕ:G1→G2\phi: G_{1} \rightarrow G_{2} is a homomorphism of groups, and N1⊲G1,N2⊲G2N_{1} \triangleleft G_{1}, N_{2} \triangleleft G_{2} are two normal subgroups, show that the map ϕ‾\bar{\phi} given on cosets by ϕ‾(xN1)=ϕ(x)N2\bar{\phi}\left(x N_{1}\right)=\phi(x) N_{2} is a well-defined homomorphism ϕ:G1/N1→G2/N2\phi: G_{1} / N_{1} \rightarrow G_{2} / N_{2} of quotient groups if and only if the original homomorrphism satisfies ϕ(N1)⊆N2\phi\left(N_{1}\right) \subseteq N_{2}.
(2) Show that if pp is a prime and HH is a subgroup of GG, then the number of distinct pp-Sylow subgroups of HH is less than or equal to the number of distinct pp-Sylow subgroups of GG.
(3) How many nonisomorphic abelian groups are there of order 325 ?
(4) If xx is an element of a PID RR, show that the left RR-module R/RxR / R x is irreducible as a module if and only if the element xx is irreducible as an element.
(5) How many non-similar linear transformations TT can there be on an 8 -dimensional real vector space VV having the minimum polynomial μT(t)=(t−2)(t−3)(t−6)3\mu_{T}(t)=(t-2)(t-3)(t-6)^{3} ? List their Jordan canonical forms.
(6) Consider the transformation TT on ℝ3\mathbb{R}^{3} which rotates points around the zz-axis through a fixed angle θ\theta.
(a) Find the matrix of TT with respect to the canonical basis for ℝ3\mathbb{R}^{3}.
(b) Find the characteristic polynomial of TT, and factor it into irreducibles (ignore the cases when θ\theta is an integer multiple of π\pi ).
(c) Find the Jordan canonical form for TT (you may have to pass to complex matrices).
(7)(a) Show that for any two elements a,ba, b in a cyclic group, at least one of the 3 elements a,b,aba, b, a b is a square (i.e. is of the form x2x^{2} for some element xx in the group).
(b) Show that the polynomial f(x):=(x2−2)(x2−3)(x2−6)f(x):=\left(x^{2}-2\right)\left(x^{2}-3\right)\left(x^{2}-6\right) has no integral roots in ℤ\mathbb{Z}, but for any prime p>0p>0 it has integral roots modulo pp (i.e. the congruence f(x)≡0mod⁡pf(x) \equiv 0 \bmod p has an integral solution).
(8) Find the Galois group of f(x):=x13−1f(x):=x^{13}-1 over the rationals ℚ\mathbb{Q} (i.e. Gal⁡(K/ℚ)\operatorname{Gal}(K / \mathbb{Q}) for KK the splitting fields of f(x)f(x) over ℚ\mathbb{Q} ).
