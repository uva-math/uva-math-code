Algebra General Exam August 22, 1998

This is exam is worth 100 points. The number of points each part of a problem is worth is indicated in brackets. If you can’t prove part of some problem, you can still use the result of that part in proving subsequent parts.
(1)[20] Let GG be a finite group of order 3⋅5⋅173 \cdot 5 \cdot 17.
(a) [5] Show that the Sylow 17-subgroup is normal.
(b) [5] If there exists an element of order 15 in GG, show that the Sylow 3- and 5-subgroups are also normal. [Hint: show they are properly contained in their normalizers.]
(c)[5] If all Sylow subgroups of a finite group GG are normal and abelian, show that GG itself is abelian.
(d)[5] If GG is a finite abelian group of order p⋅q⋅rp \cdot q \cdot r for distinct primes p,q,rp, q, r, show that GG is cyclic.
(2)[15] Let GL2(p)G L_{2}(p) for a prime pp denote the group of invertible 2×22 \times 2 matrices over the finite field FpF_{p} of pp elements.
(a) [5] Find the order nn of the group GL2(p)G L_{2}(p).
(b) [5] For λ\lambda in FpF_{p}, the find the order mm of the subgroup

Gλ={A∈GL2(p)|A(λ10λ)A−1=(λ10λ)}G_{\lambda}=\left\{A \in G L_{2}(p) \left\lvert\, A\left(\begin{array}{cc} \lambda & 1 \\ 0 & \lambda \end{array}\right) A^{-1}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} \lambda & 1 \\ 0 & \lambda \end{array}\right)\right.\right\}

(c)[5] Find how many 2×22 \times 2 matrices over FpF_{p} are similar to the matrix (λ10λ)\left(\begin{array}{cc}\lambda & 1 \\ 0 & \lambda\end{array}\right). [Hint: express it in terms of mm and nn.]
(3)[10] For an abelian group AA, the dual group A*A^{*} is defined to be Hom⁡(A,U)\operatorname{Hom}(A, U) where the circle group UU is the multiplicative group of complex numbers of modulus 1 (the unit circle in the complex plane). Here Hom⁡(A,B)\operatorname{Hom}(A, B) denotes the abelian group of homomorphisms of AA into BB (under (f+g)(a)=f(a)+g(a)(f+g)(a)=f(a)+g(a) ); you may use the additivity property Hom⁡(A1⊕A2,B)≅Hom⁡(A1,B)⊕Hom⁡(A2,B)\operatorname{Hom}\left(A_{1} \oplus\right. \left.A_{2}, B\right) \cong \operatorname{Hom}\left(A_{1}, B\right) \oplus \operatorname{Hom}\left(A_{2}, B\right) and the isomorphism property that if A≅A′,B≅B′A \cong A^{\prime}, B \cong B^{\prime} then Hom⁡(A,B)≅Hom⁡(A′,B′)\operatorname{Hom}(A, B) \cong \operatorname{Hom}\left(A^{\prime}, B^{\prime}\right).
(a) [5] Prove that Zn*Z_{n}^{*} is cyclic of order nn.
(b)[5] Prove that A*A^{*} is isomorphic to AA for any finite abelian group AA.
(4)[10] Let a,b,ca, b, c be distinct elements of an integral domain DD. Show that there are unique elements x,y,zx, y, z in DD such that

x+y+z=0ax+by+cz=0\begin{array}{ll} x+y+z & =0 \\ a x+b y+c z & =0 \end{array}

for all elements a,ba, b of RR. Show that if DD is a derivation, and in addition D2=0D^{2}=0 and RR has no 2-torsion ( 2a=02 a=0 implies a=0a=0 ), then the "exponential map" Id+DI d+D is an automorphism of RR.
(6)[15] Let VV be an nn-dimensional vector space over the complex numbers, and let TT be a linear transformation from VV to itself whose minimum polynomial μ(x)\mu(x) has degree 2 .
(a) [5] Find all possible Jordan Canonical Forms for TT. [Hint: consider the possible factorizations of μ(x)\mu(x) over KK.]
(b)[5] Show that VV is a direct sum of TT-invariant subspaces, each of which has dimension less than or equal to 2 .
(c) [5] Show that TT has an eigenvalue λ\lambda such that the λ\lambda-eigenspace (the set of all eigenvectors for the eigenvalue λ\lambda, together with the zero vector) has dimension at least n/2n / 2.
(7)[10] Suppose that KK is a finite Galois extension of the rational field QQ which contains 3\sqrt{3} and has cyclic Galois group Gal⁡(K/Q)\operatorname{Gal}(K / Q). Show that L=Q(3)L=Q(\sqrt{3}) is the only quadratic extension of QQ contained in KK.
(8)[10] (a)[5] If FF denotes a field and ZZ the ring of integers, decide which of the following rings are PIDs and which are UFDs (no proofs are necessary):

If RR is a PID, find its Krull dimension.
