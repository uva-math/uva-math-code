Topology General Exam
August 16, 2010

Name:

Instructions: This is a four hour exam and 'closed book'. There are eight problems.

    Problem 1

  1. (a) Let T⊂ℝ5T \subset \mathbb{R}^{5} be a closed subspace homeomorphic to ℝ2\mathbb{R}^{2}. Explain why TT will be a retract of ℝ5\mathbb{R}^{5}.
    (b) View SnS^{n} as ℝn∪{∞}\mathbb{R}^{n} \cup\{\infty\}, so that the open subsets of SnS^{n} containing ∞\infty are precisely the complements of compact subsets of ℝn\mathbb{R}^{n}. Recall that a continuous function f:ℝm→ℝnf: \mathbb{R}^{m} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{n} is called proper if f−1(C)f^{-1}(C) is compact in ℝm\mathbb{R}^{m} whenever CC is compact in ℝn\mathbb{R}^{n}. Show that such a proper map extends uniquely to a continuous function f‾:Sm→Sn\bar{f}: S^{m} \rightarrow S^{n}.
    (c) With TT as in part (a), check that the inclusion i:T↪ℝ5i: T \hookrightarrow \mathbb{R}^{5} is proper. By contrast, show that no retraction r:ℝ5→Tr: \mathbb{R}^{5} \rightarrow T can be proper. (Hint: start by using part (b).)

    2. Problem 2

  2. Let ℝ∞\mathbb{R}^{\infty} denote the union ℝ↪ℝ2↪ℝ3↪…\mathbb{R} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{R}^{2} \hookrightarrow \mathbb{R}^{3} \hookrightarrow \ldots, with the union topology, i.e. U⊂ℝ∞U \subset \mathbb{R}^{\infty} is open iff U∩ℝnU \cap \mathbb{R}^{n} is open in ℝn\mathbb{R}^{n} for all nn. Let ℝω\mathbb{R}^{\omega} denote the product of a countable number of copies of ℝ\mathbb{R}, with the product topology. Check that the evident set theoretic inclusion i:ℝ∞→ℝωi: \mathbb{R}^{\infty} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{\omega} is continuous, but is not a homeomorphism onto its image.

    3. Problem 3

  3. (a) Describe a connected double cover of ℝP2∨ℝP2\mathbb{R} P^{2} \vee \mathbb{R} P^{2}. (There is more than one correct answer.)
    (b) What are the homology groups of your double cover?
    (c) What is the fundamental group of your double cover?

    4. Problem 4

  4. Let MM be the compact surface with boundary circle CC as pictured:
    (a) Explain why MM is homotopy equivalent to a figure eight. (Hint: MM is the torus with a disk removed, and the torus is often represented as a square with opposite edges identified.)
    (b) Explain why the inclusion i:C↪Mi: C \hookrightarrow M induces the zero homomorphism from H1(C)H_{1}(C) to H1(M)H_{1}(M).
    (c) By contrast, explain why ii is not null homotopic.

    5. Problem 5

  5. Suppose given a commutative diagram of abelian groups

    with exact rows and columns. Show that there are isomorphisms ker⁡α≃ker⁡β\operatorname{ker} \alpha \simeq \operatorname{ker} \beta \quad and coker⁡α≃coker⁡β\quad \operatorname{coker} \alpha \simeq \operatorname{coker} \beta.

    6. Problem 6

  6. Recall that an nn-dimensional manifold is a Hausdorff topological space MM that can be covered by open sets homeomorphic to open sets in ℝn\mathbb{R}^{n}. Prove that a compact nn-dimensional manifold can be embedded in (i.e. is homeomorphic to a subset of) ℝN\mathbb{R}^{N} for large enough NN. (Hint: use a partition of unity associated to a finite open cover U1,…,UkU_{1}, \ldots, U_{k} of MM equipped with embeddings fi:Ui→ℝnf_{i}: U_{i} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{n}.)

    7. Problem 7

  7. Let C⊂ℝ3C \subset \mathbb{R}^{3} be the union of the xx-axis and the yy-axis. Compute H*(ℝ3−C)H_{*}\left(\mathbb{R}^{3}-C\right). (Hint: note that ℝ3−C=(ℝ3−x\mathbb{R}^{3}-C=\left(\mathbb{R}^{3}-x\right.-axis )∩(ℝ3−y) \cap\left(\mathbb{R}^{3}-y\right.-axis )).)

    8. Problem 8

  8. Let XX be a Hausdorff space, and f:X→Xf: X \rightarrow X a continuous function such that

  • f(x)≠xf(x) \neq x for all x∈Xx \in X, and

  • f∘ff \circ f is the identity.
    (a) Show that every x∈Xx \in X has an open neighborhood WxW_{x} satisfying f(Wx)∩Wx=∅f\left(W_{x}\right) \cap W_{x}=\emptyset.
    (b) Let X‾=X/(x∼f(x))\bar{X}=X /(x \sim f(x)), with the quotient space topology. Show that the quotient map q:X→X‾q: X \rightarrow \bar{X} is a covering map.
