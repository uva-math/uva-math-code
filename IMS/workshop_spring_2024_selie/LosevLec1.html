Quantum Groups and Their Forms

Notation: g\mathfrak{g} simple Lie algebra/ℂ\mathbb{C}, simply laced (ADE) — to simplify the exposition.

Generic Version

Definition 1 (Quantum group Uv(g)U_v(\mathfrak{g})). Uv(g)U_v(\mathfrak{g}) is ℂ(v)\mathbb{C}(v)-algebra w. generators EiE_i, FiF_i (i∈Ii \in I), KνK_\nu (ν∈Λ\nu \in \Lambda) & relations:

  • KνKν′=Kν+ν′K_\nu K_{\nu'} = K_{\nu+\nu'}, K0=1K_0 = 1

  • KνEiKν−1=v(ν,αi)EiK_\nu E_i K_\nu^{-1} = v^{(\nu, \alpha_i)} E_i, KνFiKν−1=v−(ν,αi)FiK_\nu F_i K_\nu^{-1} = v^{-(\nu, \alpha_i)} F_i

  • [Ei,Fj]=δi,jKαi−Kαi−1v−v−1\left[E_i, F_j\right] = \delta_{i,j} \frac{K_{\alpha_i} - K_{\alpha_i}^{-1}}{v - v^{-1}}

  • quantum Serre relations: EiEj=EjEiE_{i} E_{j}=E_{j} E_{i} if aij=0a_{ij}=0 EiEj2+Ej2Ei=(v+v−1)EjEiEj if aij=−1E_{i} E_{j}^{2}+E_{j}^{2} E_{i}=\left(v+v^{-1}\right) E_{j} E_{i} E_{j} \text{ if } a_{ij}=-1 & same for FiF_i’s

Forms and Specializations

We want to consider ℂ[v±1]\mathbb{C}[v^{\pm 1}]-forms of Uv(g)U_{v}(\mathfrak{g}) i.e. ℂ[v±1]\mathbb{C}[v^{\pm 1}]-subalgebras A⊂Uv(g)A \subset U_{v}(\mathfrak{g}) w. ℂ(v)⊗ℂ[v±1]A→∼Uv(g)\mathbb{C}(v) \underset{\mathbb{C}[v^{\pm 1}]}{\otimes} A \xrightarrow{\sim} U_{v}(\mathfrak{g})

To define the forms we need we introduce some notation. n∈ℤ≥0⤳[n]v=vn−1+vn−3+…+v−(n−1)n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0} \leadsto [n]_{v}=v^{n-1}+v^{n-3}+\ldots+v^{-(n-1)}, a quantum integer [n]v!=∏i=1n[i]v[n]_{v}!=\prod_{i=1}^{n}[i]_{v} Ei(n)=Ein/[n]v!E_{i}^{(n)}=E_{i}^{n} /[n]_{v}! (divided power), same for Fi(n)F_{i}^{(n)}.

Definition 2 (Forms of Uv(g)U_v(\mathfrak{g})).

  • The De Concini-Kac form UvDK(g)U_{v}^{DK}(\mathfrak{g}) is the ℂ[v±1]\mathbb{C}[v^{\pm 1}]-subalgebra generated by EiE_{i}’s, FiF_{i}’s & KνK_{\nu}’s (the most obvious form).

  • The Lusztig form UvlU_{v}^{l}: by Ei(n),Fi(n),KνE_{i}^{(n)}, F_{i}^{(n)}, K_{\nu} (∀i∈I,ν∈Λ,n∈ℤ≥0)(\forall i \in I, \nu \in \Lambda, n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0})

  • The mixed form UvmixU_{v}^{mix}: Ei(n),Fi,KνE_{i}^{(n)}, F_{i}, K_{\nu} (∀i∈I,ν∈Λ,n∈ℤ≥0)(\forall i \in I, \nu \in \Lambda, n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0})

The point of considering the forms is that one can specialize vv to q∈ℂ×q \in \mathbb{C}^{\times} getting algebras over ℂ\mathbb{C}, e.g. Uqmix=Uvmix⊗ℂ[v±1]ℂqU_{q}^{mix}=U_{v}^{mix} \otimes_{\mathbb{C}[v^{\pm 1}]} \mathbb{C}_{q} (ℂq\mathbb{C}_{q} is 1-dim. ℂ[v±1]\mathbb{C}[v^{\pm 1}]-algebra w. v↦qv \mapsto q). When qq is not a root of 11, [n]q≠0∀n⇒UqDK=Uqmix=Uql[n]_{q} \neq 0 \forall n \Rightarrow U_{q}^{DK}=U_{q}^{mix}=U_{q}^{l}. But when qq is primitive 1d\sqrt[d]{1} we have [d]q=0[d]_{q}=0 (if dd is even, [d/2]q=0[d/2]_{q}=0) & the specializations behave differently from each other.

There are several reasons to be interested in the specializations to q=1dq=\sqrt[d]{1} from a representation-theoretic perspective. Historically first was a connection to modular (=mod⁡p=\bmod p) representation theory of U(g)U(\mathfrak{g}) & the corresponding algebraic group GG.

The form whose representation theory resembles that of G/𝔽¯pG/\overline{\mathbb{F}}_{p} is UqlU_{q}^{l}, it’s also the best understood (since the 90’s). The form whose representations resemble those of U(g𝔽¯p)U(\mathfrak{g}_{\overline{\mathbb{F}}_{p}}) is UqDKU_{q}^{DK}, it’s least understood. For these lectures we’ll concentrate on the intermediate form, UqmixU_{q}^{mix}.

Quantum Category O\mathcal{O}

Definition

We fix an odd positive integer dd. Let ε\varepsilon be a primitive 1d\sqrt[d]{1}. Note that UvmixU_{v}^{\mathrm{mix}} & UεmixU_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}} are graded by Λ0\Lambda_{0}, Uεmix=⨁λ∈Λ0Uε,λmixU_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}=\bigoplus_{\lambda \in \Lambda_{0}} U_{\varepsilon, \lambda}^{\mathrm{mix}} deg⁡Fi=−αi,deg⁡Kν=0,deg⁡Ei(l)=lαi\operatorname{deg} F_{i}=-\alpha_{i}, \quad \operatorname{deg} K_{\nu}=0, \quad \operatorname{deg} E_{i}^{(l)}=l\alpha_{i} so it makes sense to speak about the category of graded UεmixU_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}-modules:

  • the objects are UεmixU_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}-modules MM w. direct sum decomposition M=⨁μ∈Λ0MμM=\bigoplus_{\mu \in \Lambda_{0}} M_{\mu} w. Uε,λmixMμ⊂Mλ+μU_{\varepsilon, \lambda}^{\mathrm{mix}} M_{\mu} \subset M_{\lambda+\mu} ∀\forall λ,μ∈Λ0\lambda, \mu \in \Lambda_{0}

  • morphisms are UεmixU_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}-linear maps φ:M→N\varphi: M \rightarrow N w. φ(Mμ)⊂Nμ∀μ.\varphi\left(M_{\mu}\right) \subset N_{\mu} \quad \forall \mu.

Definition 3 (Quantum category O\mathcal{O}). The quantum category O\mathcal{O}, to be denoted by Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}, is the full subcategory in the category of graded UεmixU_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}-modules consisting of all MM s.t.

  1. MM is finitely generated over UεmixU_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}},

  2. Kν|Mμ=ε(μ,ν)idMμ\left.K_{\nu}\right|_{M_{\mu}}=\varepsilon^{(\mu, \nu)} \mathrm{id}_{M_{\mu}}, where (⋅,⋅)(\cdot, \cdot) is a WW-invariant form on h*\mathfrak{h}^{*} normalized so that (α,α)=2(\alpha, \alpha)=2 ∀\forall root α\alpha — recall that we assume that g\mathfrak{g} is simply laced for simplicity.

  3. {μ∣Mμ≠0}\left\{\mu \mid M_{\mu} \neq 0\right\} is bounded from above: ∃μM∈Λ0\exists \mu_{M} \in \Lambda_{0} s.t. Mμ≠0⇒μM−μ∈Span⁡ℤ≥0(αi∣i=1,…,r)M_{\mu} \neq 0 \Rightarrow \mu_{M}-\mu \in \operatorname{Span}_{\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}}\left(\alpha_{i} \mid i=1, \ldots, r\right).

Example 1 (Verma module). λ∈Λ0⤳Δεmix(λ)=Uεmix/Uεmix(Kν−ε(ν,λ),Ei(l)∣∀i,ν,l)\lambda \in \Lambda_{0} \leadsto \Delta_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}(\lambda)=U_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}} / U_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}\left(K_{\nu}-\varepsilon^{(\nu, \lambda)}, E_{i}^{(l)} \mid \forall i, \nu, l\right) w. class of 11 in deg⁡=λ\deg = \lambda

Remarks

  1. Note that the definition makes perfect sense when ε\varepsilon is not a root of 11. In this case (ii) can be made into the definition of MμM_{\mu}: ε(ν,μ1)=ε(ν,μ2)∀ν⇒μ1=μ2\varepsilon^{(\nu, \mu_{1})}=\varepsilon^{(\nu, \mu_{2})} \forall \nu \Rightarrow \mu_{1}=\mu_{2}. So the category Oε\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon} (we omit the superscript "mix" b/c there’s no difference between the three specializations) is the complete analog of the classical BGG category O\mathcal{O} of representations of simple Lie algebras.

  2. A counterpart of Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}} for modular representations is as follows. Let 𝔽=𝔽‾p\mathbb{F}=\bar{\mathbb{F}}_{p}, B𝔽⊂G𝔽B_{\mathbb{F}} \subset G_{\mathbb{F}} be a Borel subgroup. We can talk about (g𝔽,B𝔽)(\mathfrak{g}_{\mathbb{F}}, B_{\mathbb{F}})-modules: U(g𝔽)U(\mathfrak{g}_{\mathbb{F}})-modules MM equipped in addition w. a rational representation of B𝔽B_{\mathbb{F}} s.t. the action map U(g𝔽)⊗M→MU(\mathfrak{g}_{\mathbb{F}}) \otimes M \rightarrow M is B𝔽B_{\mathbb{F}}-linear & the differential of the B𝔽B_{\mathbb{F}}-action coincides w. the action of b𝔽⊂g𝔽\mathfrak{b}_{\mathbb{F}} \subset \mathfrak{g}_{\mathbb{F}}. The modular analog of Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}} consists of all (g𝔽,B𝔽)(\mathfrak{g}_{\mathbb{F}}, B_{\mathbb{F}})-modules finitely generated over U(g𝔽)U(\mathfrak{g}_{\mathbb{F}}).

  3. As usual, each Verma Δεmix(λ)\Delta_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}(\lambda) has a unique irreducible quotient, to be denoted Lεmix(λ)L_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}(\lambda) & λ↦Lεmix(λ):Λ0→∼Irr⁡(Oεmix)\lambda \mapsto L_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}(\lambda): \Lambda_{0} \xrightarrow{\sim} \operatorname{Irr}(\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}). One can show that dim⁡Lεmix(λ)<∞\operatorname{dim} L_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}(\lambda)<\infty. On the other hand, Δεmix(λ)\Delta_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}(\lambda) is infinite dimensional (in fact its formal character is the same as of the usual Verma). It follows that objects in Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}} may have infinite length. But they are Noetherian — in fact, each object in Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}} admits a finite filtration by quotients of Verma modules.

Block Decomposition of Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}

It turns out that the category Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}} introduced last time splits into the direct sum of subcategories called blocks (a more precise name would be "infinitesimal blocks"). To define the blocks we’ll need an affine Weyl group WaW^{a} and its suitable action on Λ0\Lambda_{0}, the root lattice of g\mathfrak{g}.

We set Wa=W⋉Λ0W^{a}=W \ltimes \Lambda_{0}. We write tμt_{\mu} for μ∈Λ0\mu \in \Lambda_{0} viewed as an element of WaW^{a}. Define an action of WaW^{a} on Λ0\Lambda_{0} by w⋅λ:=w(λ+ρ)−ρ(as usual ρ=12∑α>0α)tμ⋅λ:=λ+dμ(where d is the order of ε)\begin{aligned} w \cdot \lambda &:= w(\lambda+\rho)-\rho \quad \text{(as usual } \rho=\tfrac{1}{2} \textstyle\sum_{\alpha>0} \alpha\text{)}\\ t_{\mu} \cdot \lambda &:= \lambda+d \mu \quad \text{(where } d \text{ is the order of } \varepsilon\text{)} \end{aligned}

Definition 4. Let Θ\Theta be a WaW^{a}-orbit in Λ0\Lambda_{0}. Define the full subcategory OεΘ⊂Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\Theta} \subset \mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}} as consisting of all objects MM that admit a (finite) filtration by quotients of Δε(λ),λ∈Θ\Delta_{\varepsilon}(\lambda), \lambda \in \Theta.

Proposition 1. Oεmix=⨁ΘOεΘ\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\text{mix}}=\bigoplus_{\Theta} \mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\Theta}.
