Quantum Category O\mathcal{O}, II

Affine Hecke Category

We have seen that the affine Weyl group controls certain aspects of Oεmix\mathcal{O}_\varepsilon^{\mathrm{mix}}. In fact, much more is true: there are derived equivalences between OεΘ\mathcal{O}_\varepsilon^{\Theta}’s & certain “standard” categories associated to WaW^a: (singular) blocks of the affine Hecke category.

Example 1. W′=WaW' = W^a, V=h⊕ℂℏV = \mathfrak{h} \oplus \mathbb{C}\hbar, Wa↷VW^a \curvearrowright V: ℏ\hbar is WaW^a-invariant, WW acts on h\mathfrak{h} as usual, and for λ∈Λ0\lambda \in \Lambda_0, have tλx=x+⟨λ,x⟩ℏt_\lambda x = x + \langle \lambda, x \rangle \hbar, x∈hx \in \mathfrak{h}.

Definition 1. Let s∈Ss \in S. Define the Bott-Samelson RR-bimodule Bs=R⊗RsRB_s = R \otimes_{R^s} R (where RsR^s is the subalgebra of ss-invariants in RR). Note that BsB_s is graded as a bimodule.

For j∈ℤj \in \mathbb{Z}, let ⟨j⟩\langle j \rangle denote the endofunctor of grading shift: B⟨j⟩i=Bi+j(for B∈R-gr(bi)mod)B\langle j\rangle_i = B_{i+j} \quad (\text{for } B \in R\text{-gr(bi)mod}) We consider the following (additive) categories:

  • SBim (the category of Soergel bimodules): the full subcategory in RR-grbimod consisting of all graded bimodules obtained from RR & BsB_{s}, s∈Ss \in S, by ⊗\otimes, ⊕\oplus, ⟨j⟩\langle j \rangle, & taking direct summands.

  • SMod (the category of Soergel modules), the full subcategory {B/BV∈R-grmod∣B∈SBim}\{B / BV \in R\text{-grmod} \mid B \in \text{SBim}\}. One can show it’s closed under taking direct summands.

  • SMod_\underline{\text{SMod}} (the category of ungraded Soergel modules), a full subcategory in RR-mod whose objects are the same as in SMod_\underline{\text{SMod}} (w. grading forgotten).

The categories SBim, SMod & SMod_\underline{\text{SMod}} are "Krull-Schmidt": every object is isomorphic to a unique ⊕\oplus of indecomposables. The Soergel categorification theorem describes the indecomposable objects in these categories, we will need a version for SMod_\underline{\text{SMod}}.

Proposition 1. The indecomposable objects in SMod_\underline{\text{SMod}} are in bijection w. W′W'

Derived Equivalence, Regular Blocks

Theorem 1 (I.L. 23). Suppose Θ\Theta is a free WaW^{a}-orbit (no stabilizer). Then Db(OεΘ)→∼ℋD^{b}\left(\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\Theta}\right) \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathcal{H}.

A somewhat weaker version of Thm was also obtained by R. Situ.

Remark 1. Such orbit exists ⇔\Leftrightarrow d>hd > h (h=h = Coxeter number of GG, e.g. for G=SL⁡nG = \operatorname{SL}_n, h=nh = n). In this case Θ:=Wa⋅0\Theta := W^{a} \cdot 0 is free, the corresponding block is called principal.

Derived Equivalence, Singular Blocks

Consider the general (not necessarily free) Θ\Theta. We want a description of OεΘ\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\Theta} similar to that in Theorem 1. First, we’ll choose a distinguished element λ0∈Θ\lambda^{0} \in \Theta. Set A={λ∈Λ0∣⟨λ+ρ,αi∨⟩≤0&⟨λ+ρ,δ∨⟩≥−d}\mathcal{A} = \left\{\lambda \in \Lambda_{0} \mid \langle\lambda+\rho, \alpha_{i}^{\vee}\rangle \leq 0 \;\&\; \langle\lambda+\rho, \delta^{\vee}\rangle \geqslant -d\right\}, where δ∨\delta^{\vee} is the maximal coroot. Then A\mathcal{A} is a fundamental domain for Wa↷Λ0W^{a} \curvearrowright \Lambda_{0} (exercise). λ0:=\lambda^{0} := unique point in A∩Θ\mathcal{A} \cap \Theta.

For SS take the set of reflections about the walls of A\mathcal{A}. Set J={s∈S∣s⋅λ0=λ0}J = \left\{s \in S \mid s \cdot \lambda^{0} = \lambda^{0}\right\}. RJ=⋂s∈JRsR^{J} = \bigcap_{s \in J} R^{s}, polynomial algebra.

Motivations

Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}} was popularized by Gaitsgory who wanted an extension of the famous Kazhdan-Lusztig equivalence between

  1. a certain parabolic category O\mathcal{O} for the affine Lie algebra ĝ\hat{\mathfrak{g}}

  2. and the finite dimensional representations of UεlU_{\varepsilon}^{l}

He proposed to replace (i) w. the full cat. O\mathcal{O} & Uεl-modfdU_{\varepsilon}^{l}\text{-}\mathrm{mod}_{fd} w. Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}. His conjectures essentially yield our main equivalence & were proved by his students Chen & Fu by methods very different from mine (& with different assumptions on dd). The motivation came from Quantum Geometric Langlands.

Another motivation: one should be able to use a connection between Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}} and the affine Hecke category to get character formulas in the category of finite dimensional of UεDKU_{\varepsilon}^{DK} with some (currently a bit mysterious) additional “equivariance” structure, similar in spirit to Bezrukavnikov-I.L. 20.

Finally, a far reaching motivation is to understand the affine analog of Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}: where instead of Uεmix(g)U_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}(\mathfrak{g}) one has Uεmix(ĝ)U_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}}(\hat{\mathfrak{g}}) — understanding Oεmix\mathcal{O}_{\varepsilon}^{\mathrm{mix}} is a necessary prerequisite.
